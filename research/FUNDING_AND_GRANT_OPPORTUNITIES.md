# Pentary Computing: Funding and Grant Opportunities

This document outlines funding sources, grant programs, and financial strategies for developing Pentary computing technology.

---

## Executive Summary

Pentary computing can pursue funding through multiple channels:

| Channel | Amount Range | Timeline | Probability |
|---------|--------------|----------|-------------|
| **Government Grants** | $100K - $10M | 6-18 months | Medium |
| **Industry Partnerships** | $500K - $50M | 3-12 months | Medium |
| **Venture Capital** | $2M - $100M | 3-6 months | Low-Medium |
| **Angel/Seed** | $100K - $2M | 1-3 months | Medium |
| **Crowdfunding** | $50K - $500K | 2-4 months | Medium |
| **Academic Grants** | $50K - $1M | 6-12 months | Medium-High |

**Recommended Strategy:** Start with academic grants and government programs, then pursue industry partnerships as technology matures.

## 1. US Government Funding Programs

### 1.1 National Science Foundation (NSF)

#### Computing and Communication Foundations (CCF)
- **Program**: Core research in computing
- **Relevance**: Multi-valued logic, novel architectures
- **Typical Award**: $200K - $500K (3 years)
- **Deadline**: Annual (varies by program)
- **URL**: https://www.nsf.gov/div/index.jsp?div=CCF

**Relevant Programs:**
| Program | Amount | Focus |
|---------|--------|-------|
| CAREER | $500K-$600K | Early-career researchers |
| Small | $500K | Exploratory research |
| Medium | $1.2M | Larger-scope projects |
| FuSe | $1.5M | Future semiconductors |

#### Convergence Accelerator
- **Focus**: Translational research
- **Amount**: Phase 1: $750K, Phase 2: $5M
- **Relevance**: AI hardware innovation
- **URL**: https://www.nsf.gov/od/oia/convergence-accelerator/

### 1.2 DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)

#### Electronics Resurgence Initiative (ERI)
- **Focus**: Novel computing paradigms
- **Amount**: $1M - $50M
- **Relevance**: Post-Moore computing
- **URL**: https://www.darpa.mil/work-with-us/electronics-resurgence-initiative

**Relevant Programs:**
| Program | Focus | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|
| BEOL | Back-end integration | Active |
| FRANC | ReRAM computing | Active |
| DREaM | Analog computing | Active |
| OPTIMA | Memory optimization | Active |

#### AI Exploration (AIE)
- **Focus**: Novel AI approaches
- **Amount**: $250K - $1M
- **Timeline**: 18-month projects
- **Relevance**: New AI hardware

### 1.3 Department of Energy (DOE)

#### Office of Science - ASCR
- **Focus**: Advanced Scientific Computing Research
- **Amount**: $200K - $5M
- **Relevance**: Novel computing for science
- **URL**: https://science.osti.gov/ascr

#### ARPA-E
- **Focus**: Energy-efficient computing
- **Amount**: $500K - $10M
- **Relevance**: Power efficiency claims
- **URL**: https://arpa-e.energy.gov/

### 1.4 CHIPS and Science Act Programs

#### CHIPS R&D
- **Focus**: Semiconductor research
- **Amount**: Variable (part of $39B)
- **Relevance**: Domestic chip innovation
- **Status**: Programs being established

#### National Semiconductor Technology Center (NSTC)
- **Focus**: Collaborative R&D
- **Relevance**: Novel architecture research
- **URL**: https://www.nist.gov/chips

### 1.5 Small Business Programs

#### SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)
| Agency | Phase I | Phase II | Phase III |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| NSF | $275K | $1M | Unlimited |
| DOD | $250K | $1.5M | Unlimited |
| DOE | $250K | $1.5M | Unlimited |

#### STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- **Requirement**: University partnership
- **Advantage**: Academia-industry bridge
- **Relevance**: Pentary academic collaborations

## 8. Funding Strategy Recommendations

### 8.1 Phase-Based Funding Plan

```
Phase 0: Foundation ($50K-$100K)
├── Source: University seed grant, angel investor
├── Duration: 3-6 months
├── Goal: Validate concept, build team
└── Deliverable: Working simulation, preliminary results

Phase 1: Research Validation ($200K-$500K)
├── Source: NSF, SBIR Phase I, SRC
├── Duration: 12-18 months
├── Goal: Academic credibility
└── Deliverable: Publications, FPGA demo

Phase 2: Prototyping ($1M-$3M)
├── Source: SBIR Phase II, DARPA, Series Seed
├── Duration: 18-24 months
├── Goal: Silicon demonstration
└── Deliverable: chipIgnite tapeout, test chip

Phase 3: Productization ($5M-$20M)
├── Source: Series A, strategic investment
├── Duration: 24-36 months
├── Goal: Product development
└── Deliverable: Production chip, software stack

Phase 4: Scale ($20M-$100M)
├── Source: Series B, corporate partners
├── Duration: 24-36 months
├── Goal: Market entry
└── Deliverable: Commercial products, revenue
```

### 8.2 Parallel Funding Tracks

**Track A: Academic/Government**
- NSF grants
- DARPA programs
- DOE funding
- Timeline: 6-18 months

**Track B: Industry**
- SRC programs
- Corporate research
- Intel INRC
- Timeline: 3-12 months

**Track C: Commercial**
- Angel investment
- Venture capital
- Strategic partners
- Timeline: 3-6 months

### 8.3 Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Grant rejection | Apply to multiple programs |
| VC not interested | Focus on government first |
| Long timelines | Parallel applications |
| Technical failure | Stage-gate milestones |
| Market shift | Flexible roadmap |

## 9. Application Tips

### 9.1 NSF Grant Writing

**Key Elements:**
1. Clear intellectual merit
2. Broader impacts
3. Preliminary results
4. Strong team credentials
5. Realistic timeline/budget

**Common Mistakes:**
- Too incremental
- Overpromising
- Weak broader impacts
- Budget mismatch

### 9.2 DARPA Proposal Strategy

**Success Factors:**
1. Disruptive potential
2. Technical approach clarity
3. Team credibility
4. Clear metrics
5. Government relevance

**BAA Response:**
- Read BAA carefully
- Address all requirements
- Highlight innovation
- Include contingencies

### 9.3 VC Pitch Elements

**Deck Structure:**
1. Problem (1 slide)
2. Solution (1 slide)
3. Technology (2-3 slides)
4. Market (1-2 slides)
5. Competition (1 slide)
6. Business model (1 slide)
7. Team (1 slide)
8. Financials (1 slide)
9. Ask (1 slide)
